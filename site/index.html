Leadership Philosophy

My Leadership Philosophy

"I try to lead the way Pop coaches — high standards for everyone, accepted because people know it comes from genuine care for them as whole humans, not just cogs in the machine."

Pop won five championships not just by demanding results, but by earning trust. He held every player accountable — from Hall of Famers to the end of the bench — because they knew the coaching came from a place of genuine care. I bring that same approach to every SE I lead: the bar is high, feedback is direct, and my investment in each person's growth is real.

The best sales engineers lead with hospitality — it's the one competitive edge that can never be lost. They show up consistently, not just when they need something, and when they don't have an answer, they say so and follow up promptly. Above all, they think long-term. Sales is a marathon, and the strongest performers know how to read the race — pushing hard through critical stretches, then pacing themselves enough to do it again.

Gregg Popovich

“El Jefe”, San Antonio Spurs
